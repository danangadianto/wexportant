Palm oil bulls to roar into 2022 on labor crunch and La Nina deluge

Palm oil bulls will likely hold sway into early 202 as a chronic worker shortage in No. 2 grower Malaysia and unusually heavy rains in key growing areas disrupt production of the world’s most-consumed edible oil.

That could keep prices elevated after a record year in 2021 when benchmark futures rocketed to an all-time closing high of 5,071 ringgit ($1,1213) a ton.

Although Malaysia is trying to expedite the arrival of much-needed foreign workers for its plantations, the spread of omicron may scupper those plans, constrain harvesting and keep supplies tight.

In the first quarter, they may be attempts to reach new highs around 5,200 ringgit and beyond, as supply tightness persists at a time of low seasonal production and festive demand for Lunar New Year, said Gnanasekar Thiaggarajan, head of trading and hedging strategies at Kaleesuwari Intercontinental in Mumbai. “But in subsequent quarters. Prices will be pressured by good supplies and could decline to 3,975 ringgit or even lower.”
